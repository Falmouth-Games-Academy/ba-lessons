This session introduces you to several aspects of law that will be relevant to computing professionals and independent game developers. Particularly, those laws which will be of importance to programmers operating in commercial contexts. Owing to the nature of the creative industries and the work associated with this particular module, our exploration will have particular focus on several aspects of intellectual property. Notably, those associated with software copyright, trademark, design rights, and patents.


Learning Objectives:
  • Define law
  • Explain the difference between copyright, trademark, design right, and patent
  • Explain software licencing
  • Analyse the legal considerations you should make for your group and individual projects
  • Suggest an appropriate license for an open-source project

Session Materials:

A Brief Introduction to English Law for Game Developers

Activity (5 minutes) --- Note, in your own words, a definition of 'law'.

Defining Law

Activity (5 minutes) --- Review your definition of 'law' and note how it differs to the definition provided in the video.

Activity (10 minutes) --- Discuss with a partner what 'law' means and how this definition could be better tailored to distinguish criminal law from laws relating to the video games industry.

Historic Lens

Activity (10 minutes) --- Discuss with a partner whether it is useful to allow courts to make law.

Sources of Law

Activity (10 minutes) --- In pairs, identify and list at least FOUR sources of law that might be relevant to games.

Civil vs Criminal Law

Activity (10 minutes) --- Research and discuss with a partner, whether a game-related case has been heard in a criminal court.

Activity (10 minutes) --- Note why the "balance of probabilities" is used in the civil courts.

Copyright

Activity (20 minutes) --- Identify and note at least FIVE parts of a game which can be protected by copyright and FIVE parts that cannot be protected by copyright. You may work as a pair.

Trademarks

Activity (10 minutes) --- Discuss which parts of one of your recent game projects could be trademarked. You may work as a pair.

Design Rights

Activity (10 minutes) --- Discuss with a partner why the design of software (i.e., the code and its architecture) is not protected by design right.

Patents

Activity (10 minutes) --- Identify and note TEN video game patents. You may work collaboratively with your peers.

Stretch Activity (Remaining Time) --- Identify an open-source game on GitHub. For example, Barotrauma. Discuss the implications of releasing open-source games for EACH of copyright, trademark, design right, and patent. Then, discuss a license that would faciliate continued commercial use of the project, despite being open and available. Note its implications and contrast the potential advantages and disadvantages.


Further Learning:
  • Review Lambarth's "Really Basic Introduction to English Law and the English Legal System"
  • Consider the impact of Patent Trolls on industry
  • Read an Analysis of Clone Games and Fan Games
  • Explore Open-Source Licenses
